Walter Ciszek

(1904-1984)

  • Jesuit priest, Russian prisoner of war, and author
  • Born in the Shenandoah, Pennsylvania to a Polish immigrant family
  • Former gang member, turned Jesuit priest
  • Moved to Soviet Russia to serve as a missionary
  • Went to Rome to study theolgy, Russian language, and Russian history
  • Sent to a Jesuit mission in Poland
  • Captured by the Soviet Union, tortured, forced to work for over 20 years
  • Wrote multiple books
"The fullest freedom I had ever known, the greatest sense of security, came from abandoning my will to do only the will of God."
